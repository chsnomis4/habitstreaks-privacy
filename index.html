Habit Streaks
Privacy Policy — Last updated May 2026

🔒 Habit Streaks does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything stays on your device.

Overview

Habit Streaks is a personal habit tracking app. We built it with a simple principle: your personal data belongs to you, and only you. The app works entirely offline and stores all data locally on your device.

What data does the app store?

Habit Streaks stores the following information locally on your device:

  • Habit names and types you create
  • Daily habit completion records
  • Journal entries you write
  • Fasting session records
  • App preferences (dark mode, timezone, etc.)

This data is stored using your device's local storage and never leaves your device.

Data collection

We do not collect any data. Habit Streaks has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, no crash reporting, and no third-party SDKs that transmit data. There is no registration or sign-in required.

Data sharing

We do not share any data with third parties because we do not have access to your data. Your habit records, journal notes, and personal information are stored exclusively on your device.

Data export and backup

The app provides a built-in JSON export feature so you can back up your data at any time. Exported files are saved to your device and are under your full control. We recommend regular exports to protect against data loss if you change devices or reinstall the app.

Data deletion

You can delete all app data at any time by uninstalling the app. Individual habits and their history can also be deleted from within the app using the habit settings menu. Deleting a habit permanently removes all associated data from your device.

Children's privacy

Habit Streaks does not collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. The app contains no advertising, no in-app purchases that require personal information, and no social features.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change how the app handles data, we will update this page and revise the date above. Any future changes that involve data collection will be opt-in and clearly disclosed.
